Black Sabbath members:

Ozzy Osbourne - vocal

(1948-2025) Ozzy Osbourne, also known as the "Prince of Darkness", is one of the most charismatic and controversial figures in rock history. Born in 1948 in Birmingham, he rose to fame as the frontman of the legendary band Black Sabbath, often credited as the pioneers of heavy metal. After leaving the group, he launched a highly successful solo career with hits like "Crazy Train", "Mr. Crowley", and "No More Tears." He became famous not only for his powerful voice and dark image but also for his eccentric behavior — including the infamous incident where he bit the head off a bat on stage. Despite a turbulent life and numerous health struggles, Ozzy remains a rock icon and a symbol of raw energy and rebellious spirit.

Tony Iommi - lead guitar

Tony Iommi, often called the "Godfather of Heavy Metal", is the legendary guitarist and founding member of Black Sabbath. Born in 1948 in Birmingham, England — the same industrial city as Ozzy Osbourne — Iommi helped shape the dark, heavy sound that would define metal music for generations. Despite losing the tips of two fingers in a factory accident at age 17, he refused to give up playing guitar; instead, he crafted homemade prosthetics and tuned his instrument lower to ease the tension, accidentally creating the iconic heavy, doom-laden riffs that became Black Sabbath's signature. As the band's main composer, Iommi wrote countless classics such as "Iron Man", "Paranoid", and "War Pigs". His creativity, resilience, and unmistakable guitar tone have inspired thousands of musicians and earned him a place among the greatest guitarists in rock history.

Geezer Butler - bass guitar

Geezer Butler, the bassist and primary lyricist for Black Sabbath, is widely regarded as one of the most influential bass players in heavy metal history. Born in 1949 in Birmingham, England, he helped define the genre's dark and heavy sound with his deep, driving bass lines and thought-provoking lyrics inspired by religion, war, and social issues. Geezer's powerful style often mirrored Tony Iommi's guitar riffs, creating the thick, dark tone that became Black Sabbath's trademark. Beyond his musicianship, he brought a poetic and philosophical edge to the band's songs. His innovative use of effects and aggressive finger-style playing have influenced countless bassists, cementing his legacy as one of metal's true pioneers.

Bill Ward - drums

Bill Ward, the drummer and founding member of Black Sabbath, is celebrated for his powerful yet soulful drumming style that helped lay the foundation of heavy metal. Born in 1948 in Birmingham, England, Ward combined jazz-inspired rhythms with hard-hitting power, giving Black Sabbath's music its distinctive groove and intensity. Beyond his drumming, Bill also contributed backing vocals and occasional lead vocals, showcasing his versatility as a musician. Despite facing personal struggles throughout his career, his influence on rock and metal percussion is immense, and he remains revered as one of the genre's most innovative and passionate drummers.
